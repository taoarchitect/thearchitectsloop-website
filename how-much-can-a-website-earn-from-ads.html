Website Monetization Guide

How Much Can a Website Earn From Ads?

A practical guide to website ad revenue, RPM, traffic requirements, geography, device mix, and realistic earning expectations.

Clear Answer

A website can earn a few dollars or thousands per month from ads.

The main factors are traffic volume, visitor country, website niche, device mix, page views per visitor, and the ad network used.

Simple example: a small website with 10,000 monthly visitors may earn around $10–$200/month. A larger website with 100,000+ monthly visitors may earn several hundred to several thousand dollars per month.

Basics

How website ad revenue works

Most websites earn money through display advertisements. Advertisers pay ad networks to show ads on websites, and website owners receive part of that advertising revenue.

  • More visitors usually create more earning potential.
  • Visitors from higher-value advertising markets usually increase revenue.
  • Some niches attract higher advertiser demand.
  • Desktop traffic can sometimes earn more than mobile traffic.
  • More page views per visitor can increase ad impressions.

RPM

RPM explained without jargon

RPM means revenue per 1,000 page views. It is a simple way to estimate how much a website might earn from traffic.

Example:

If your RPM is $5 and your website gets 100,000 page views:

100 × $5 = $500 estimated revenue

These are simplified examples, not guaranteed earnings.

Traffic Quality

Why geography and device mix matter

Advertisers pay different amounts depending on where visitors are located. Traffic from countries with larger advertising budgets often generates higher revenue.

Device type also matters. Desktop visitors can sometimes generate higher ad revenue because larger ad placements are possible, while mobile traffic may behave differently depending on layout and audience behavior.

Limitations

Important limitations

Website ad revenue is unpredictable. Search traffic can change, ad rates can move seasonally, and not every visitor will see or interact with ads.

Many websites also earn money from sponsorships, affiliate offers, memberships, consulting, or digital products. Ads are only one possible revenue stream.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

How much traffic do I need to make money from ads?

Some websites begin earning small amounts with a few thousand monthly visitors, but meaningful income usually requires consistent traffic growth.

What is a good RPM?

RPM varies heavily by niche and audience location. Some websites may see under $1 RPM, while others can exceed $20 RPM.

Can small websites make money?

Yes. Small websites can generate revenue, especially in high-value niches or with targeted audiences.

Is ad revenue passive income?

Not entirely. Websites usually require content updates, SEO work, maintenance, and traffic generation.
